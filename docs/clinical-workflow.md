# AI--Nursing Clinical Workflow Design

## Overview

AI--Nursing is designed around the real workflow of nurses in acute care environments. The system supports assessment, prioritization, documentation, communication, and clinical decision-making while keeping the nurse as the primary decision-maker.

The goal is to provide the right information at the right time without adding unnecessary cognitive burden.

---

# 1. Beginning of Shift: Patient Overview

At the beginning of a shift, AI--Nursing organizes available patient information into a clear clinical picture.

Information sources may include:

- Electronic health record data
- Vital signs
- Laboratory results
- Medication information
- Device information
- Nursing assessments
- Provider orders
- Trends over time

The system creates a concise patient overview while allowing the nurse to verify information.

---

# 2. Dynamic Patient Visualization

The nurse can view the patient through an interactive clinical dashboard.

Possible features:

- Visual representation of patient status
- Body-system overview
- Device tracking
- Medication visualization
- Wound and skin assessment locations
- Lines, drains, and airway information

The nurse can select areas of interest to quickly access relevant information.

---

# 3. Risk Identification and Prioritization

AI--Nursing analyzes patterns and identifies areas requiring attention.

Examples:

- Changes in vital signs
- Worsening laboratory trends
- Increasing oxygen requirements
- Medication concerns
- Possible deterioration patterns
- Missed care priorities

The system prioritizes information rather than creating excessive alerts.

---

# 4. Predictive Timeliness Engine

The predictive timeliness engine evaluates trends and estimates when attention may be needed.

Potential capabilities:

- Detect early warning patterns
- Identify subtle changes over time
- Prioritize nursing actions
- Consider patient complexity
- Consider nurse workload and competing priorities

The purpose is to provide earlier awareness, not replace clinical judgment.

---

# 5. Nurse Assessment and Clinical Judgment

The nurse remains responsible for:

- Patient assessment
- Clinical interpretation
- Decision-making
- Communication
- Interventions

AI--Nursing provides recommendations and organized information, but the nurse determines appropriate action.

---

# 6. Documentation Support

The system assists with documentation by:

- Organizing assessment findings
- Reducing repetitive charting
- Helping maintain accurate timelines
- Supporting completion of required documentation

The nurse reviews and approves all documentation.

---

# 7. Provider Communication

AI--Nursing can help identify when provider communication may be appropriate.

Potential features:

- Summarized patient changes
- Relevant trends
- Supporting clinical information
- Suggested discussion points

The nurse maintains control over communication decisions.

---

# 8. Continuous Learning and Improvement

The system evaluates outcomes to improve future support.

Potential learning areas:

- Accuracy of predictions
- Effectiveness of alerts
- Workflow improvements
- User feedback

Clinical safety and transparency remain priorities.

---

# Future Vision: Digital ICU Twin

A future version of AI--Nursing may include a digital ICU twin capable of representing a patient's current condition and possible future states.

Potential uses:

- Education
- Clinical planning
- Risk visualization
- Scenario modeling
- Improved team communication

---

# Core Design Principle

AI--Nursing exists to enhance the nurse's ability to provide safe, compassionate, evidence-based care.

The technology should reduce burden, improve awareness, and strengthen—not replace—the human connection at the center of nursing.
